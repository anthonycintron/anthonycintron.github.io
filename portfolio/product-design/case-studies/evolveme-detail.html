EvolveMe, a digital platform that enables teens to take part in career explorations connected to critical transferrable skills.


Objective

Our responsibility at Devito/Verdi was to design and develop a digital experience that motivates students to learn about their education and career options by rewarding them for completing productive tasks.

2 min Sizzle Reel

Watch this video case study if you are press for time.

Solution

A digital student-led career and readiness experience. Launched a digital experience which helps kids gain awareness of education pathways and career possibilities that match their interests, increasing access to valuable skill sets, and giving them the confidence that they need to navigate a path to success after high school.



Market Research

EvolveMe was co-created with a nationwide panel of middle and high school teen advisors who provided feedback on design, site features and functionality, UX, and naming, along with quantitative validation based on feedback from over 4,600 additional young people.


Like all generations, Gen Z has developed a visual lexicon on their own, which is largely influenced by nostalgia, social media motifs, and playful use of color. All conceptual approaches utilize this visual language to resonate with the youth of today.


UX Research

User testing revealed that while most youth found the imagery eye catching, the colors leaned too feminine/childish. We refined our art direction to ensure we hit the mark.


User Persona Development

There were three trending types of personas while researching and interviewing students.

  • Incentive-driven teen—those who lightly engage in self-betterment tasks and are willing to do them in exchange for desirable rewards.
  • Validation seekers— those who are already moderately practicing self betterment tasks and want to be able to prove and share their progress with adults, peers, and mentors.
  • Self-growth teen— students already heavily practicing self-betterment tasks and seeking accountability and progress tracking.

UX Objectives

EvolveMe gives youth daily building tools to begin their adulthood with confidence. We divided our user experience objectives into three key categories.


Measurable Results

Following are results within 9 months of initial launch:


Sweepstakes Program

  • Users earn points for activities and tasks
  • Once user earned 1k points, they are eligible to enter a weekly sweepstakes drawing
  • Two weekly winners selected randomly to win $5k per winner
  • One grand prize winner at end of year


Digital Rewards Program

When tasks are completed, teens can earn points to redeem gift cards to their favorite retail, restaurant, and entertainment brands.


Partnership Program

The EvolveMe platform features 16 career experimentation and skill-building activities and 130 sub-tasks from 20+ innovative partners that serve millions of teens.


Award Winning

The Anthem Awards is the largest and most comprehensive social impact award. With over 2,000 submissions from 44 countries around the world, 10,000+ reviews from jurors, and over 25,000 supporters in the Anthem Community Voice, the 3rd Annual Anthem Award Winners were announced on Jan 30, 2024.

EvolveMe was announced as the gold and silver winner of the 2024 Education, Art & Culture Education, Product, Innovation Categories.
